# Sprint 2 Product Review Guard - 2026-03-29

## Alignment verdict
**Approved with reservations.**

Sprint 2 now reads much more like REVORY Seller than like an import shell with premium skin. Activation, booking path, and revenue baseline are noticeably stronger, and the center of gravity is closer to lead-to-booking. The remaining drift is not structural scope creep; it is mostly a perception issue in some dashboard empty states where source/setup language still competes too hard with the booking promise.

## Strong alignment points
- Setup is narrower and more activation-driven.
- `Main offer`, `lead source`, `booking path`, and `deal value` now behave like true booking pillars.
- Revenue is no longer framed as a decorative number; it is tied to visible bookings plus a defined deal value.
- Seller does not read like CRM, inbox, chatbot, or heavy ops tooling in the reviewed Sprint 2 surfaces.
- Unsupported source breadth was reduced during this review, which protects MVP honesty.

## Findings
### P2 - Zero-state dashboard still over-explains the source layer
- The dashboard hero and follow-on sections still give heavy visual weight to `source`, `feed`, and import state when there is no data yet.
- Why this is off-strategy:
  The source layer should feed the booking motion, not compete with it as a perceived product center.
- Safer MVP read:
  Keep source language present but quieter than the lead-to-booking promise and revenue outcome.

### P3 - A few words still lean older than Seller
- Isolated copy still uses `operational` framing in setup/onboarding explanations.
- Why this is off-strategy:
  Seller should sound like booking acceleration, not a renamed operations layer.
- Safer MVP read:
  Prefer `booking`, `live Seller`, `booked outcome`, or `revenue path`.

## Scope guard
- No evidence of CRM drift in the reviewed Sprint 2 flow.
- No evidence of inbox drift in the reviewed Sprint 2 flow.
- No evidence of chatbot-open drift in the reviewed Sprint 2 flow.
- No evidence of feature inflation that would force a redesign or broader MVP scope.

## Recommendation
Sprint 2 can move forward as **approved with reservations**.

The sprint now supports the Seller thesis well enough to stand, especially after removing unsupported source options from activation. The next cleanup should stay small: reduce source prominence in zero-data dashboard states and remove the last bits of ops-flavored copy. No rebuild is needed.

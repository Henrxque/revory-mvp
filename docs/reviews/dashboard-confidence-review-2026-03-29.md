# Dashboard Confidence Review

Date: 2026-03-29

## Alignment Verdict

The dashboard is now more clearly aligned with the REVORY Seller thesis. It opens as a premium revenue-first booking surface, keeps the main number dominant, and explains that number with just enough commercial proof to feel credible in demo and sales contexts. The page no longer leans as heavily toward generic operational reading.

## Findings

- The previous dashboard top still had some strong booking-motion language, but not enough executive framing. That made the first read slightly more operational than commercial.
- The support cards below the hero were still giving space to future signals like response time and advance rate. Those are honest, but they weakened confidence because they are not the strongest proof for a revenue-first demo.
- Mid-page sections still contained operational framing such as `Lead-to-booking motion`, `Booking inputs`, and `Next leverage point`, which made the page feel closer to a guided workspace than an executive sales surface.
- The revenue proof area duplicated the revenue read too literally. That diluted the dominance of the main number instead of supporting it.
- Some labels in visibility sections still sounded import-admin oriented rather than revenue-proof oriented.

## Scope Adjustments

- No new modules were added.
- No analytics, filters, or BI behaviors were introduced.
- No redesign or structural rebuild was performed.
- The refinement stayed inside hierarchy, copy, labels, section framing, and supporting visual emphasis.

## What Was Refined

- Hero framing now opens as `Executive revenue read` instead of a motion-first read.
- The main revenue card now reinforces proof with `Booked appointments` and `Value per booking` directly under the dominant number.
- The first support row now prioritizes:
  - booked appointments
  - value per booking
  - main offer live
  - booking path
- The revenue-proof section now explains why the number holds without duplicating the main KPI as another equal card.
- `Next leverage point` was moved into the executive support block as `Next commercial move`, which reduced mid-page operational weight.
- `Lead-to-booking motion` was simplified into `Revenue path` with only three proof stages:
  - lead base
  - booking path
  - booked outcome
- `Booking inputs` was reframed as `Booked visibility`.
- Upload-row labels were rewritten from admin wording to revenue-proof wording such as `Visible rows`, `Needs review`, and `Rows received`.
- `Booking context` became `Commercial context`.
- `Appointments in view` became `Upcoming booked appointments`.
- `Secondary signals` became `Signals that turn on later`, making it explicit that they are supportive, not central.

## Copy Adjustments

- Prefer `revenue`, `booked proof`, `commercial context`, and `booked visibility` over broader operational language.
- Keep `next move` short and commercial, not queue-like.
- Keep upload/import language only where it is functionally necessary.
- Keep secondary signals honest and demoted so they do not compete with the executive number.

## Final Verdict

Approved.

The dashboard now feels more mature, more commercially legible, and more sale-ready while preserving the REVORY Seller revenue-first thesis and avoiding CRM or BI drift.
